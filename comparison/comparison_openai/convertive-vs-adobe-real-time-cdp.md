# Convertive vs Adobe Real-Time CDP

## Bottom line

Adobe Real-Time CDP is an enterprise-grade customer data platform for unified profiles, audience management, governance, and activation. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer wants a focused ecommerce system for in-session intervention instead of a large enterprise data foundation.

## Where Adobe RT-CDP is strong

Adobe's strength is data unification at scale: real-time profiles, pseudonymous and known data, governance, activation, and omnichannel readiness. It is a serious option for complex enterprises with multiple brands, channels, teams, and compliance needs.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive can win by being much closer to the operator and much closer to the conversion event. Teams do not buy Convertive to solve enterprise data governance. They buy it to convert live traffic better. That is a sharper use case, easier to budget against, and easier to measure quickly.

| Buying question | Convertive | Adobe Real-Time CDP |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main job | Convert anonymous visitors in-session | Unify customer data and activate audiences at enterprise scale |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce growth and digital commerce team | Enterprise data, marketing ops, and Adobe-centric organizations |
| Strength | Session intent and immediate intervention | Real-time profiles, governance, and activation breadth |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if you need a conversion system, not a large data foundation | Better if you need a governed enterprise CDP first |
| Time-to-value story | Faster and more focused | Longer, broader, and more architectural |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a "buy the outcome, not the platform category" message. If the outcome is more governed data, Adobe RT-CDP fits. If the outcome is more anonymous visitors converted before bounce, Convertive has the stronger story.
