# Convertive vs Dynamic Yield

## Bottom line

Dynamic Yield is a broad enterprise personalization and experimentation platform. Convertive is the better choice when a brand wants to stay focused on anonymous visitor conversion, in-session next-best-action, and fast time-to-value around one high-value ecommerce problem.

## Where Dynamic Yield is strong

Dynamic Yield positions itself as an experience OS with segmentation, targeting, recommendations, journey orchestration, optimization, search, and enterprise integrations. It is built for teams running large personalization programs across many channels and surfaces.

## Why choose Convertive instead

That breadth is also the opening for Convertive. Convertive can win when the buyer does not need a giant experience platform. Its story is more focused: real-time profiles, in-session intent, anonymous-to-known capture, next-best-action selection, and reporting tied to live conversion quality.

| Buying question | Convertive | Dynamic Yield |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main use case | Live-session conversion of anonymous traffic | Enterprise-wide personalization and optimization |
| Product center of gravity | In-session decisioning and identity progression | Experience orchestration across content, products, offers, and testing |
| Best fit team | Lean ecommerce growth and digital teams | Large enterprise personalization and experimentation teams |
| Why buyers may prefer it | Clearer thesis, less platform sprawl, stronger anonymous-conversion story | Larger breadth, deeper experimentation, more mature enterprise footprint |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if you want one platform narrative around converting intent before bounce | Better if you need a full experience optimization suite |

## Recommended blog angle

Position Convertive as the specialist and Dynamic Yield as the suite. That creates a clean decision frame: choose the suite if you are building a broad experience program, choose Convertive if the revenue leak is anonymous traffic that leaves without action.
