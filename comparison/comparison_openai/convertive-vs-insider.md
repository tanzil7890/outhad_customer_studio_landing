# Convertive vs Insider

## Bottom line

Insider One is a large AI-powered customer engagement suite spanning CDP, personalization, journeys, reporting, and many channels. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer wants a tighter ecommerce thesis centered on anonymous visitor conversion in the live session.

## Where Insider is strong

Insider's official story is broad: AI, CDP, personalization, journey orchestration, reporting, behavioral analytics, and channel coverage from web to WhatsApp to app to search. It is compelling for teams replacing several enterprise engagement vendors at once.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive can win by avoiding that breadth trap. If a brand's real problem is not "we need one giant customer engagement cloud," but "we are losing too much value from anonymous visitors before we can act," Convertive is easier to buy, easier to message internally, and more clearly tied to ecommerce revenue.

| Buying question | Convertive | Insider |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Core job | Convert anonymous visitors before they bounce | Run broad AI-powered customer engagement across channels |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce operator or growth lead | Enterprise CRM or digital engagement leader |
| Strength | In-session intent, next-best-action, identity progression | Broad omnichannel coverage plus CDP and AI |
| Risk | Less broad than enterprise suites | Can feel like more platform than needed for the specific problem |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if you want focus on ecommerce conversion moments, not a full multichannel operating system | Better if consolidation across many channels is the top goal |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a "focus beats sprawl" narrative. Insider is broad. Convertive is specific. If the board-level question is how to recover more value from anonymous traffic right now, Convertive has the tighter answer.
