# Convertive vs Segment

## Bottom line

Twilio Segment is a CDP built for collection, unification, audience building, and downstream activation. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer needs a product that converts anonymous traffic in-session rather than a platform that mainly organizes customer data.

## Where Segment is strong

Segment's public positioning centers on collecting real-time customer data, creating unified profiles, building audiences, and orchestrating journeys across the Twilio ecosystem and other destinations. It is powerful for data standardization and profile infrastructure.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive should be framed as the missing decision and action layer. A CDP can tell you who the customer is. Convertive is positioned to decide what to do while the user is still on the site, before you lose the chance to influence the session.

| Buying question | Convertive | Segment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main job | Convert live anonymous traffic | Collect, unify, and activate customer data |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce conversion team | Data platform and CRM teams |
| Core value | In-session next-best-action | Unified profiles and activation infrastructure |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if the KPI is recovered sessions and anonymous conversion lift | Better if the KPI is data standardization and profile availability |
| Coexistence | High | High |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a "CDP is not enough" story. Segment is valuable infrastructure, but infrastructure alone does not save the session. Convertive is the layer built for that moment.
