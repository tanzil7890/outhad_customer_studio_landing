# Convertive vs Klaviyo

## Bottom line

Klaviyo is a B2C CRM built around email, SMS, data, analytics, and service. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer wants to solve the problem that happens before Klaviyo can do its best work: the visitor is still anonymous and still on-site.

## Where Klaviyo is strong

Klaviyo is very strong for owned-channel marketing once identity exists. It combines CRM, messaging, analytics, and data into a marketer-friendly platform and remains a default choice for ecommerce lifecycle teams.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive should not try to sound like a better ESP. It should sound like the product that creates more reachable customers, more in-session conversions, and better-timed identity capture for whatever lifecycle platform comes next. That makes Convertive a strong front-end conversion layer rather than a downstream messaging system.

| Buying question | Convertive | Klaviyo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main job | Convert and identify visitors during the session | Engage known customers and subscribers across owned channels |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce conversion and site experience teams | CRM and lifecycle marketing teams |
| Strength | In-session intent and anonymous-to-known progression | Email, SMS, forms, segmentation, lifecycle automation |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if the problem starts before the email is captured | Better if the customer is already known and ready for lifecycle orchestration |
| Partnership potential | Very high | Very high |

## Recommended blog angle

This should be one of the strongest pieces in the set. The frame is simple: Klaviyo is what happens after the visitor is reachable. Convertive is how you create more reachable and convertible visitors in the first place.
