# Convertive vs mParticle

## Bottom line

Rokt mParticle is a CDP focused on real-time identity resolution, audience activation, and hybrid activation across stream and warehouse data. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer needs an ecommerce conversion system, not another data platform.

## Where mParticle is strong

mParticle's public positioning emphasizes real-time identity resolution, unified profiles, AI-powered audiences, activation across channels, and flexibility across real-time and warehouse-native architectures. It is designed for brands that need governed customer data activation at scale.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive sits closer to the revenue event. Its story is not "unify and activate data everywhere." Its story is "watch the current session, understand the intent, and intervene before the visitor disappears." That difference matters when the business problem is anonymous-session loss rather than architecture maturity.

| Buying question | Convertive | mParticle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main job | Convert anonymous session traffic | Unify and activate customer data across systems |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce growth leader | Data platform and customer data team |
| Strength | Live-session next-best-action and identity capture | Identity resolution, profile unification, audience activation |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if the team wants a decision and orchestration layer | Better if the team wants a hybrid CDP foundation |
| Relationship | Complementary in some stacks | Complementary in some stacks |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a "CDP vs conversion layer" contrast. mParticle helps make customer data usable. Convertive is what you buy when you want that usability to change what happens before a shopper leaves.
