# Convertive vs Braze: The Web Session Layer Braze Doesn't Own

> Braze is brilliant at engaging the customers you already have. Convertive creates customers from the anonymous visitors Braze has never seen.

## The Core Difference

Braze is one of the most respected customer engagement platforms in the industry. Built mobile-first, it excels at coordinating push notifications, in-app messages, email, SMS, and content cards for brands with significant native app user bases. If you're an enterprise running a loyalty app with millions of active users, Braze's segmentation depth, Canvas journey builder, and BrazeAI capabilities are genuinely powerful.

The challenge for most Shopify merchants is that Braze was architected around a different problem: re-engaging known customers through a mobile app. The anonymous web visitor on a Shopify storefront — browsing without logging in, without a profile, without an app installed — is a population that Braze's architecture isn't built to serve.

Convertive is built precisely for that population. It handles the web session layer: the anonymous first-time visitor who lands on your Shopify store from an Instagram ad, browses three products, hesitates at the cart, and either converts, identifies themselves, or leaves. Every second of that session is Convertive's territory. Braze enters the picture only after that visitor has been identified — and only if they have your app installed.

For Shopify merchants operating primarily through a web storefront, Convertive and Braze address genuinely different problems. Convertive converts sessions. Braze re-engages customers.

## Where Braze Falls Short for Shopify Web Conversion

Braze's limitations on the Shopify storefront aren't product failures — they're the natural result of having been built for a different platform (native mobile apps) and a different customer segment (enterprise brands with millions of app users).

### 1. Braze Has No Presence on the Shopify Storefront

Braze operates through its SDKs — iOS, Android, web JavaScript. For Braze's web SDK to function meaningfully, it needs a logged-in user or a recognized profile. An anonymous visitor to a Shopify store, browsing without logging in, is invisible to Braze's personalization layer. There's no mechanism to show them a contextual overlay, a behavioral product recommendation, or an exit-intent popup based on their current session behavior.

Convertive's entire value proposition lives in this anonymous web session space. Every visitor, known or unknown, gets a live behavioral profile from their first click. The system monitors intent signals in real time and acts on them — on the page, while the session is live.

### 2. Engineering-Heavy Implementation

Braze implementations are not self-service. Getting Braze connected to a Shopify store, mapping ecommerce events to Braze's data model, building the first Canvases, and configuring segmentation typically requires weeks of engineering work and significant platform expertise. Braze's power is proportional to the implementation investment.

Convertive's setup for a Shopify store is a JavaScript snippet and a campaign configuration in the Command Center — accessible to non-technical marketing teams without engineering resources.

### 3. The Problem Braze Solves Requires Braze to Have Your Customer First

Braze's journey orchestration, Canvas flows, and AI personalization are all predicated on having an identified customer with a profile. The challenge for most Shopify merchants isn't "how do we engage our existing customers better?" — Braze answers that question well. The challenge is "how do we convert the 80% of visitors we've never seen before?" That's Convertive's problem.

Every contact that Convertive captures and identifies during a session becomes a potential Braze user. Convertive fills Braze's funnel from the anonymous web layer that Braze can't reach.

### 4. Primarily Mobile-First in Architecture and Investment

Braze's product investment, feature depth, and core differentiation centers on mobile experiences: push notifications, in-app messages, app-side behavioral flows, content cards. For Shopify merchants whose customers primarily interact through a web browser — not a native app — much of Braze's most compelling capability is simply irrelevant.

## What Convertive Does Differently

### Owns the Anonymous Web Session

Convertive's architecture is built for the web storefront first. Every anonymous session is a data stream that feeds a live profile. Every behavioral signal — page views, scroll depth, product clicks, cart additions, hesitation patterns — is processed in real time to determine the optimal next action. This happens for every visitor, regardless of whether they're logged in or have ever interacted with you before.

### From Unknown to Known, In Session

The most important conversion event for many Shopify merchants isn't a purchase — it's the moment an anonymous visitor provides their email address. Convertive's progressive profiling system is built to maximize this capture rate: timing the identity prompt to intent signals, using frictionless UX patterns, and stitching the anonymous session history into the known profile instantaneously. This creates the identified customer that Braze can then engage.

### Complementary in the Stack

For enterprise Shopify brands running a native mobile app alongside their web store, Convertive and Braze serve different but complementary layers. Convertive converts anonymous web sessions and captures identity. Braze engages those identified customers through the mobile app, push notifications, and complex omnichannel journeys. The handoff from Convertive to Braze at the point of identification creates a seamless cross-platform experience.

### Speed and Simplicity

For Shopify merchants who don't have a native app, don't have a dedicated engineering team, and need to move their conversion metrics this quarter — Convertive provides the immediate, self-serve path to in-session personalization that Braze's enterprise architecture can't match in time-to-value.

## The Bottom Line

Braze is exceptional at what it was built for: re-engaging and retaining known customers through mobile-first experiences. For enterprise brands with native apps and millions of identified users, it's a foundational platform that drives measurable loyalty and lifetime value improvements.

But Braze doesn't solve the Shopify merchant's most urgent conversion problem: the anonymous web visitor who browses and leaves without ever entering Braze's system. For a storefront where 70–80% of sessions are from people Braze has never seen, Braze's value doesn't activate until the problem Convertive solves has already been addressed.

Convertive is the conversion engine that creates the customer relationships Braze then maintains. For Shopify merchants without a native app, Convertive IS the customer engagement layer. For those building toward a full omnichannel experience that includes a native app, Convertive and Braze together cover the complete customer lifecycle — from anonymous first visit to loyal returning customer.
