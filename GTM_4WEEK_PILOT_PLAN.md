# 4-Week Pilot Customer Acquisition Plan
> Goal: 3–5 Founding Customers in 4 weeks

---

## Why LinkedIn + Cold Email Failed

| Problem | Root Cause |
|---|---|
| Wrong channel | DTC/Shopify founders barely use LinkedIn. They live on Twitter/X, private Slacks, Discord, and podcasts |
| Wrong persona | Marketing managers don't approve tools. You need Founders/CEOs of DTC brands doing $500K–$5M revenue, or Head of Growth |
| Wrong format | This product is **visual**. Text email cannot convey "we identified an anonymous visitor as price-sensitive and fired a personalized popup." You need video |
| No social proof | Without a demo, case study, or real numbers, there is nothing to believe |

---

## Before Anything Else: 2 Assets (Days 1–3)

### Asset 1 — 90-Second Loom Demo on a Real Store

Record this exact sequence:

1. Anonymous visitor lands on store
2. Their live profile building in real time (category affinity, scroll depth, price sensitivity score)
3. Exit intent fires a **personalized** popup — not a generic one
4. Visitor converts / submits email
5. Profile stitches: anonymous → known

> This single video replaces 10 cold emails. Post it everywhere before doing any outreach.

**Where to post it:**
- Twitter/X thread (primary)
- Reddit posts (inline)
- Every DM you send
- Facebook group posts
- Your email signature

---

### Asset 2 — Personalized ROI Calculator

A simple calculator you fill in per prospect before sending a DM.

**The formula:**
```
Monthly Visitors × (1 - Conv. Rate) = Anonymous Sessions Lost
Anonymous Sessions Lost × Lift % = New Customers Recovered
New Customers Recovered × AOV = Revenue Recovered / Month
Revenue Recovered × 12 = Annual Impact
```

**Example message using it:**

> "Your store has ~12,000 monthly visitors. At 2.3% conversion, that's 11,724 anonymous sessions leaving every month. If we lift by just 0.4% = 48 extra customers/month. At $65 AOV = **$3,120 recovered revenue/month**. Pilot is free for 60 days."

**Personalized link format (if you build the calculator page):**
```
https://roi.tryconvertive.com?store=BrandName&visitors=12000&rate=2.3&aov=65
```

> This math creates urgency. It makes the cost of **not** trying feel painful.

## Pilot Framing — Say This, Not That

| ❌ Don't Say | ✅ Say Instead |
|---|---|
| "pilot" | **Founding Customer Program** |
| "testing our product" | "building alongside us" |
| "free trial" | "Free for 60 days — we do the full setup" |
| "can I get your feedback?" | "30 min/week call — you get direct access to our founding team" |

**The exact framing to use in every message:**

> "We're not looking for paying customers right now. We're looking for **5 Shopify stores to build alongside us**. Free for 60 days. We do the entire setup — zero work on your end. In return, we ask for a 30-minute feedback call per week and a testimonial if it works."

This makes them feel like **co-builders, not test subjects**.

## Week 4 — ProductHunt + Direct Installs (Days 22–28)

### ProductHunt Launch

- Launch on: **Tuesday or Wednesday** (avoid Monday, Friday)
- URL: `producthunt.com`

**What ProductHunt gives you:**

| ✅ Does | ❌ Doesn't |
|---|---|
| 200–800 early adopter signups (day of) | Directly bring DTC store founders |
| High-DA backlink (SEO value) | Drive sustained traffic |
| Press pickup (TechCrunch, TLDR, newsletters watch PH) | Replace community outreach |
| "As seen on ProductHunt" credibility badge | — |

**Use the launch as outreach ammunition:**

```
"We just launched on ProductHunt today and are looking for
3 Shopify stores to pilot for free. Here's the link: [PH URL]"
```

This makes cold outreach feel timely and gives prospects a reason to look now.

---

### Shopify App Store — Direct Install (Don't Wait)

> You do **NOT** need App Store approval to get pilot customers.

**Direct install:** Send prospects this URL pattern:
```
https://THEIR-STORE.myshopify.com/admin/oauth/authorize?client_id=YOUR_API_KEY&scope=...&redirect_uri=...
```

They click, authorize, done. No App Store listing needed.

**In parallel:** Submit to the Shopify App Store now — it takes 2–6 weeks for review. The App Store is a **long-game** channel, not a Week 1 channel.

## The Core Insight

> The product sells itself once someone **sees** it working.
>
> Your job right now is a **distribution problem**, not a product problem.
> Stop describing it in words. Start showing it on video.
> Stop going where investors are (LinkedIn). Start going where DTC founders are (Twitter/X, Discord, Reddit, podcasts).
