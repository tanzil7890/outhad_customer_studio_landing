# Convertive vs Rebuy

## Bottom line

Rebuy is a strong Shopify-first personalization platform focused on carts, upsells, bundles, search, flows, and AI-driven merchandising. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer wants a more cross-functional story around anonymous identity progression, next-best-action, and in-session orchestration beyond the cart.

## Where Rebuy is strong

Rebuy is purpose-built for Shopify and very strong at revenue-driving ecommerce modules: smart cart, post-purchase offers, AI-driven recommendations, search, flows, and a rules engine that adapts in real time. It is especially compelling for AOV and merchandising use cases.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive should not compete by sounding like a better Shopify cart app. It should compete by sounding like a better system for turning anonymous session signals into conversion decisions across the full journey. That is a broader value proposition than upsell logic, while still staying ecommerce-native.

| Buying question | Convertive | Rebuy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main use case | Anonymous visitor conversion and session orchestration | Shopify merchandising, cart, upsell, post-purchase optimization |
| Best fit stack | Brands wanting a more stack-level conversion layer | Shopify brands optimizing AOV and onsite monetization |
| Strength | Decisioning plus identity progression | Cart, offers, bundles, recommendations, search |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if the issue starts before cart and before identity capture | Better if the priority is Shopify-specific AOV and cart performance |
| Platform scope | More journey-led | More storefront-led |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a "before the cart vs inside the cart" frame. Rebuy is excellent when the monetization moment is already in the storefront flow. Convertive is stronger when the hard problem starts earlier.
