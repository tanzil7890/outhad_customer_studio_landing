# Convertive vs Hightouch

## Bottom line

Hightouch is a composable CDP and activation layer built around the warehouse. Convertive is the better choice when the buyer needs a real-time in-session conversion system, not just a way to activate customer data into other tools.

## Where Hightouch is strong

Hightouch is strong for audience building, identity resolution, real-time personalization hooks, journey orchestration, and syncing warehouse data into downstream systems. It is ideal for companies that want to keep data in the warehouse and activate it everywhere.

## Why choose Convertive instead

Convertive wins by being the action layer, not the pipe layer. It is positioned to decide what should happen in the current session, not only to move segments and profiles between systems. If the buyer's problem is "we have data but still miss the conversion moment," Convertive is the better answer.

| Buying question | Convertive | Hightouch |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main job | Decide and act during the session | Activate warehouse data across tools |
| Best fit buyer | Ecommerce growth operator | Data and lifecycle teams with strong warehouse maturity |
| Strength | In-session decisioning and anonymous conversion | Composable CDP and warehouse-native activation |
| Why pick Convertive? | Better if the business needs a conversion brain, not more routing | Better if the core need is data activation architecture |
| Relationship | Could complement Hightouch | Could complement Convertive |

## Recommended blog angle

Use a simple contrast: Hightouch helps you move data. Convertive helps you win the moment when that data should change a shopper's experience.
